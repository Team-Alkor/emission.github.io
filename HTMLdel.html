Step into the future with EMIT

EMIT

Invented by NASA’s best team of scientists specializing in the areas of Earth System Modeling, surface geology, mineral dust aerosols and imaging spectroscopy - EMIT finds use in many fields of study, providing us with data in order to help combat global warming. Small particles of dust are constantly being blown everywhere by the strong winds. While some may consider this unimportant, it’s significantly changing the direction of Earth’s development when it comes to global warming. Lack of knowledge of the minerals’ composition has led NASA to conduct a research, hoping to determine their impact on our planet. Depending on their color and composition, they can have a heating or cooling effect. Dark minerals contain iron causing the particles to absorb the Sun’s light (meaning they have a heating effect), while bright minerals contain clay which causes the Sun’s light to be deflected. EMIT collects light, measures in divergent wavelengths while being attached to the ISS and functions using imaging spectroscopy - an advanced technology created by NASA’s team. The human eye can see three wavelengths (green, red and blue), therefore EMIT was built to detect all of them. It was first delivered in March of 2022, but its first launch was on July 14th of 2022.
